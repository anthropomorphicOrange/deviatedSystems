New York, US


The climate of New York is continental, with cold winters and hot summers. The city is located in the eastern United States, at almost 41 degrees north latitude, on the edge of a continent that cools down in winter and heats up in summer, and where different air masses collide: the cold ones coming from Canada and the warm ones coming from the Gulf of Mexico. In the Central Park Weather Station, the daily average temperature ranges from 0.8 °C (33.4 °F) in January to 25.3 °C (77.5 °F) in July. Here are the average temperatures.

London, UK


In London, the capital of Great Britain, the climate is influenced by the ocean and is therefore cool, humid and rainy. Atlantic fronts pass one after the other throughout the year, and bring variable weather, with cloudiness, rains and showers, which alternate, at least in spring and summer, with a few hours of sunshine. At Heathrow Airport, located on the western outskirts of the city, the average temperature ranges from 5.6 °C (42 °F) in January to 19 °C (66.2 °F) in July. Here are the average temperatures.

Berlin, DE


In Berlin, the climate is moderately continental, characterized by cold winters, with average temperatures around freezing (0 °C or 32 °F), and moderately warm summers, with daytime temperatures hovering around 25 °C (77 °F). The city is the capital of Germany and is located in the north-east of the country, at 52 degrees north latitude. The city is exposed to both cold and warm air masses, so both the weather and the temperature are highly variable.
